The amaizing biodiversity of Sri Lanka

"The species diversity of Sri Lanka shows that we have 4000 species of flowering plants, 107 species of freshwater fish, 59 species of amphibians, 174 species of reptiles, 435 species of birds, 140 species of mammals and several thousand invertebrates. Within the Asian region Sri Lanka has the highest species density for flowering plants, amphibians, reptiles and mammals. Sri Lanka also has the second highest population density of humans.

Sri Lanka, nestled in the Indian Ocean, boasts an awe-inspiring of biodiversity within its compact boundaries. Renowned as a biodiversity hotspot, this island nation harbors an astonishing variety of ecosystems, from lush rainforests to arid plains and coastal wetlands. This diverse landscape is the cradle for an array of endemic species, making it a paradise for nature enthusiasts and wildlife aficionados."
